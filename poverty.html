Poverty Rates

Poverty Rates by Race

The data on poverty rates in California was collected from data.gov and aggregated by user @health on data.world (here). The data was collected from between 2000 and 2010.

As shown in the chart above, American Indian, Black American, and Hispanic populations experienced the highest rate of poverty, with the poverty rate amongst Black Americans being roughly two and a half times the poverty rate amongst Whites.

Poverty Population by Race

A combination of large population and high poverty rate results in Hispanics making up the bulk of the impoverished population in California. Given California's proximity to Mexico and significant farming industry - which provides plentiful, but low-paying, jobs for immigrants - the state is condusive to having a large Hispanic population living in poverty.

Poverty Rate by Type

The data also accounts for the different types of poverty. "Concentrated" poverty refers to the percentage of people living in poverty who live in areas in which 40% or more of people live in poverty. "Overall" poverty refers to the general population living with limited access to resources (food, education, housing, etc.).

The low concentrated poverty rate indicates that poverty in California was fairly spread out. If the concentrated poverty rate is high, it indicates that there a high degree of economic segregation.
