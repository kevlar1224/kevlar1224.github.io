HIV/AIDS Rates

HIV/AIDS Rates by Race

The data on HIV/AIDS infection rates in California was collected from HealthData.gov and aggregated by user @health on data.world. (here). The data was collected in 2012.

The data reveals that Black Americans had a far higher rate of HIV and AIDS infection than average. While most other races had infection rates around 300 cases per 100,000 people, Black Americans had over three times that rate.

HIV/AIDS Population by Race

Although Black Americans had by far the highest rate of HIV/AIDS infection, they make up a relatively small part of the population. As a result, the largest portion of the HIV/AIDS infected population is comprised by whites, the second largest population, as well as the population with the second highest infection rate.

HIV/AIDS Rates by Gender

The dataset also accounts for HIV/AIDS infection rates by gender. The infection rate among males is several times greater than the infection rate among females. The imbalance might indicate that females have more sexual partners on average (as fewer females are spreading HIV to more males), or males live longer with HIV/AIDS, or men spread HIV to other men more often than women spread to other women.
