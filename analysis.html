Analysis

Correlation Coefficient with Poverty Rate

To see how strongly the population statistics are determined by poverty, and vice versa, we can take each statistics' coefficient of correlation with poverty rate. This reveals only mild correlation with poverty rate, the highest coefficient being less than .6. So, although each of these statistics may be affected by poverty rate, all of them are influenced by additional factors. However, of all the factors, newborn disease rate is the most influenced by poverty rate.

Correlation Coefficients Between Factors

To examine further, we can find the correlation coefficients of each population statistic between each other. We see that most of these factors correlate with each other more than they do with poverty rate, with the exception of infant mortality rates with IAP application rates. Most of the coefficients are around .6, but one stands out at .972. The relation between HIV/AIDS infection rates and newborn disease rates is very linear.

Correlation Between HIV/AIDS and Newborn Diseases

Using scikit-learn, we can create a linear regression model for the relationship between HIV/AIDS infection rates and newborn disease rates. The result is a line with a slope of approximately .274 and a y-intercept around 35.042. The model has an R-squared value of .9444, which is further evidence of a linear relationship. We can see, by plotting HIV/AIDS rates against newborn disease rates, that the data points all lie very close to the line generated by the model.

The linear relationship between the two factors indicated that they either cause one another or have common underlying causes. It may be the case that HIV/AIDS infection may increase the risk of offspring developing newborn diseases (such as hypothyroidism). The two population statistics may also both be influenced by general health and health education.

For both population statistics, Black Americans lead by a large margin, whereas with other statistics, they lead by a fairly narrow margin. Their being outliers in both may be a coincidence, as the high rate of sickle cell disease in Black newborns is genetic, while HIV/AIDS infection would be influenced by outward factors such as sexual activity and access to resources such as sex education and condoms.
