Insurance Affordability Program Rates

IAP Application Rates by Race

The data on insurance affordability program (IAP) application rates in California was collected from data.chhs.ca.gov and aggregated by user @chhs on data.world (by race and by ethnicity). The data was taken from between 2016 and 2018.

While one might think that inability to afford insurance might be an accurate indicator for poverty, the reality is more complicated. The graph clearly shows that the data does not line up with the data for poverty rates. It makes sense that poverty is not enough of a determinant for need for IAPs; difficulty affording insurance could also result from pre-existing conditions or genetic predisposition for various health issues.

Conversely, a racial group may be more capable of affording insurance due to less vulnerability to illnesses. Different racial groups may also be more or less inclines to apply for IAPs due to cultural values on healthcare or seeking assistance.

IAP Application Count by Race

Again, the two dominant racial groups in terms of count are Hispanics and Whites, due to their large populations. Despite having the lowest poverty rate, Whites had one of the highest rates of IAP application.

IAP Application Rates by Age

There is also data on application rates by age, again aggregated by user data.chhs.ca.gov. It seems as people age, they become less likely to apply for IAPs, possibly as they become more established in their careers and more able to afford insurance on their own.
