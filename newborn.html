Newborn Disease Rates

Newborn Disease Rates by Race

**Note: The dataset used for this page is no longer available on data.world**

As with other categories, Black Americans are the leader with 307.39 cases of newborn diseases per 100,000 live births (almost triple the overall rate of 105.43 cases per 100,000 live births).

It is interesting to note that this chart looks quite different from the chart for infant mortality rates; particularly, mixed race infants have a very high rate of mortality, but a below-average rate of disorders. This indicates that, although disorders might reduce infant survivability, disorder rate is not a strong determinant of infant mortality.

The dataset contained no records of disorders in Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander newborns.

Newborn Disease Count by Race

Again, the two largest groups in terms of count are Hispanics and Whites, the two largest populations in California. Black Americans, despite comprising a fairly small portion of the population, had the third most cases of newborn disorders.

Most Common Diseases By Race

As the dataset contained the specific disorders that were found in newborns, we can see which disorders the different racial groups experienced. With all groups, a leading disorder was primary congenital hypothyroidism. However, it seems Black newborns experienced an incredibly high rate of sickle cell disease, which accounted for almost 90% of disorders found in Black newborns.

Disease Rate by Disease

Overall, the most prominent disorder was primary congenital hypothyroidism, with almost 1800 cases. Sickle cell disease was the second most prominent, with 566 cases, most of which were Black newborns (487 cases).
